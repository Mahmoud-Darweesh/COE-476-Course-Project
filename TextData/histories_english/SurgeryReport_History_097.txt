He has a histologically confirmed G2 squamous cell carcinoma of the larynx (cT4 cN1) on the right side. The findings were confirmed by panendoscopy <2016> in domo. A CT scan of the neck and thorax with contrast medium performed by us showed no evidence of thyroid cartilage infiltration. Secondary findings included a contrast-absorbing lesion in the area of the right vallecula and in the area of the right glossotonsillar groove. Based on the findings, the above-mentioned operation was indicated. The patient was informed in detail about the procedure and had sufficient time to ask questions. He was also presented to our phoniatrics department and had a discussion with the laryngectomy association.
